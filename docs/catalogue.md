# The catalogue of the cycle

This is the owner's handoff catalogue (requested at Phase 3), recorded verbatim. `content/cycle.json` carries only each text's navigation title and status; this page preserves the full record — Tibetan title, English title, and attribution — for every text, translated or forthcoming. Titles and author names are the owner's words, copied exactly, including their spelling variants. Nothing here is authored by the tool.

The compendium the catalogue describes:

- བར་དོ་ཐོས་གྲོལ་ཆེན་མོའི་ཆོས་སྐོར་ཕྱོགས་བསྒྲིགས། *(Tibetan text)*
- THE GREAT LIBERATION THROUGH HEARING IN THE INTERMEDIATE STATE *(English text)*

Forthcoming ids are provisional until their content lands (SCHEMA.md §2). Shelf placement below follows one rule: a text whose title names its bardo sits on that bardo's shelf; every other text sits on the Supporting Texts shelf in the owner's list order, awaiting the owner's direction. Rearranging shelves is the owner's call, not the tool's.

1. **The bilingual Liberation through Hearing spans three files.** The owner's bilingual document divides the work as three instructions — Dying, Dharmatā (peaceful and wrathful days), Becoming — so it is entered as `bardo-thodrol.dying-intro`, `bardo-thodrol.dharmata-intro`, and `bardo-thodrol.becoming-intro`, one per shelf. In the traditional catalogue the Dying material opens the ཆོས་ཉིད་བར་དོའི་ངོ་སྤྲོད་ཐོས་གྲོལ་ཆེན་མོ (entry `bardo-thodrol.dharmata-intro` here); the three-way split follows the owner's own division of the document. Confirm or redirect.
2. **Root Verses of the Six Bardos** is not a separate entry in the owner's 36-text list, but it is named in the brief, present in the owner's prayer document, and already catalogued from Phase 3 — it stays as `prayer.root-verses-six-bardos`.
3. **Author-name spellings vary in the source list** (e.g. ནམ་མཁའ་ཆོས་ཀྱི་རྒྱལ་མཚོ་ vs ནམ་མཁའ་ཚོས་ཀྱི་རྒྱལ་མཚོ་, and the English "Namkha Chökyi Gyaltsen" against Tibetan རྒྱལ་མཚོ). Copied exactly as given; normalizing them is the owner's call.
4. **Shelf placements are provisional** for every text whose title does not name its bardo — all of those sit on the Supporting Texts shelf in list order. In particular རིག་པ་ངོ་སྤྲོད་གཅེར་མཐོང་རང་གྲོལ (`ngotro.naked-seeing`) may belong with the Preliminaries, and `ngotro.purity-of-mandalas` may belong with Iconography; the owner decides.
